# The Nurtured Oven Business Model

## Recommended Operating Model

The Nurtured Oven should operate as a small-batch weekly preorder bakery centered on comfort sweets, curated boxes, and simple repeatable ordering.

The business should **not** position itself as a fully custom bakery. Custom work can exist, but only as a controlled inquiry option.

**Recommended positioning:**

> Small-batch comfort sweets, made weekly, boxed beautifully, and shared with care.

**Business model in one sentence:**

> The Nurtured Oven sells small-batch comfort sweets through a weekly preorder menu, a signature Weekly Comfort Box, curated gift boxes, seasonal drops, and limited Little Extras, using a website-to-Square ordering flow that keeps buying simple and protects production time.

---

# 1. Core Product Structure

The business should have five product categories.

## 1. Weekly Menu

The weekly menu is the recurring sales engine.

Recommended structure:

- 3 to 5 items per week
- Orders open Friday
- Orders close Wednesday at noon
- Pickup/delivery Friday
- Limited quantities available

The weekly menu should avoid too many individual options. The goal is to create demand while keeping baking manageable.

## 2. Weekly Comfort Box

This should be the hero product.

The Weekly Comfort Box is a curated variety box made from the current week’s menu items.

Why this should be the hero:

- Easier for customers to choose
- Feels giftable
- Increases average order value
- Lets her use the same weekly production
- Makes the business feel more branded and intentional
- Reduces dependence on customers choosing individual items

Recommended customer-facing description:

> A cozy mix of this week’s small-batch sweets, packed as a curated comfort box.

## 3. Gift Boxes

Gift boxes should be the premium emotional product.

Recommended gift box tiers:

| Product | Use Case | Recommended Price Zone |
|---|---|---:|
| Mini Comfort Box | Small thank-you, teacher gift, one-person gift | $16 to $22 |
| Classic Comfort Box | Main gift product, birthday, thank-you, new mom, hostess gift | $32 to $42 |
| Gathering Box | Family, office, team, small group | $55 to $75+ |

Gift boxes should mostly use weekly menu items. This keeps production simple and prevents the business from becoming chaotic.

Gift boxes should allow:

- Gift message
- Occasion note
- Pickup or delivery
- Future date request
- Limited customization by availability

Gift boxes should require advance notice unless the box is part of the weekly menu.

Recommended customer-facing description:

> Thoughtfully packed boxes of small-batch sweets for birthdays, thank-yous, teachers, new moms, hostess gifts, and everyday comfort.

## 4. Seasonal Drops

Seasonal drops should be limited-time boxes tied to holidays or moments.

Recommended seasonal calendar:

- Valentine’s Comfort Box
- Mother’s Day Box
- Teacher Appreciation Box
- Fall Comfort Box
- Thanksgiving Dessert Box
- Christmas Cookie Box

Seasonal drops should be announced early, capped, and prepaid.

## 5. Little Extras

Little Extras are limited-quantity boxes made from extra bakes, samples, test batches, or next-day treats.

Use the name **Little Extras**, not “clearance” as the main customer-facing language.

Recommended customer-facing description:

> Little Extras are limited boxes made from extra bakes, sample batches, or next-day treats. Same cozy sweets, special price, limited availability.

Little Extras should be:

- First-paid, first-claimed
- Limited quantity
- Posted only when available
- Usually sold through Square inventory or a manually updated website/social post
- Used to reduce waste, test products, and serve customers who missed the weekly cutoff

Little Extras should not become the primary brand promise.

# 2. Recommended Weekly Schedule

## Internal Schedule

| Day | Internal Activity |
|---|---|
| Friday | New menu released |
| Sunday | Off / light admin only |
| Monday | Prep / bake |
| Tuesday | Prep / bake |
| Wednesday | Orders close at noon; final ingredient and production planning |
| Thursday | Baking completed; packing begins |
| Friday | Pickup / delivery; Little Extras if available |

## Customer-Facing Schedule

> New menu drops every Friday. Orders close Wednesday at noon. Pickup and delivery happen Friday.

## Important Adjustment

The website should never feel closed.

When weekly ordering is closed, customers should still be able to:

- Join the next menu reminder list
- Request a future gift box
- Submit a future order inquiry
- Buy Little Extras if available

## Recommended Closed-Window Message

> This week’s menu is now closed so we can prep and bake everything fresh. The next menu drops Friday. Want a reminder when it opens, or need a future gift box? Send us a request below.

## Recommended Ordering Logic

| Timing | Customer Can Do |
|---|---|
| Friday to Wednesday noon | Order from weekly menu and Weekly Comfort Box |
| Wednesday noon to Friday | Join next menu reminder list, request future gift box, or watch for Little Extras |
| Friday | Pickup/delivery day; Little Extras may be available |
| Anytime | Submit future gift box inquiry |

# 3. Recommended Pricing Model

Pricing should not be positioned like a bake sale or grocery dessert aisle. The Nurtured Oven is selling small-batch comfort, convenience, giftability, and local care.

## Target Average Order Value

Recommended target:

> $25 to $40 average order value

This is high enough to make the work meaningful, but still accessible for a local comfort sweets brand.

## Recommended Price Structure

| Product | Role | Recommended Price Zone |
|---|---|---:|
| Individual treat | Low-commitment add-on or market-style item | $3.50 to $5 |
| 4-pack brownies/bars | Core weekly item | $14 to $18 |
| 6-pack cookies/bars | Core weekly item | $18 to $24 |
| Weekly Comfort Box | Hero weekly product | $28 to $38 |
| Mini Comfort Box | Small gift | $16 to $22 |
| Classic Comfort Box | Main gift product | $32 to $42 |
| Gathering Box | Larger gift/group order | $55 to $75+ |
| Little Extras Box | Limited extra/sample/day-old box | $8 to $15 |
| Seasonal Premium Box | Holiday/special occasion product | $38 to $60+ |

## Pricing Rule

Ingredient + packaging cost should usually stay around **25% to 35% of the sale price**.

This leaves room for:

- Labor
- Square fees
- Waste
- Delivery complexity
- Packaging mistakes
- Profit

## Recommendation

The website should push customers toward the Weekly Comfort Box and Classic Comfort Box, not just individual treats.

# 4. Recommended Pickup and Delivery Model

Start with both pickup and limited delivery, but keep delivery controlled.

## Recommended Fulfillment Rules

- Friday is the primary fulfillment day
- Pickup is preferred
- Delivery is available within a limited local radius
- Delivery should have either a fee or a minimum order
- Porch drop is allowed if customer approves it
- Customer must provide a safe drop-off location

## Recommended Delivery Policy

> Local delivery is available on Fridays within our delivery area. Delivery may include a small fee depending on location. If you will not be home, please provide a safe porch drop-off location.

## Delivery Recommendation

Use delivery as a convenience, not as a margin killer.

Suggested launch rule:

- Pickup: free
- Delivery: available for orders over a set amount or with a delivery fee
- Delivery route: batched on Friday, not custom times all day

# 5. Recommended Ordering and Payment Flow

## Best Launch Flow

Use this structure:

> Website menu/form → automatic customer email → Square payment → order tracker → Friday fulfillment

## Source of Truth

Recommended source-of-truth split:

| Function | Tool |
|---|---|
| Customer-facing menu and form | Website |
| Payment | Square |
| Sales reporting | Square |
| Weekly production/order management | Google Sheet or Airtable |
| Customer reminders | Email/text list later |

## Why This Is Best

Square is good for payment and reporting. The website is better for branded customer experience. A simple order tracker is better for baking and fulfillment.

## Confirmation Rule

> Orders are confirmed once payment is received.

This should be repeated in the form, confirmation email, and policy section.

## Recommended Payment Flow

1. Customer selects items on the website form.
2. Customer submits order.
3. Customer receives automatic email with order summary and Square payment instructions/link.
4. Customer pays through Square.
5. Order becomes confirmed after payment.
6. Baker sees the order in the tracker.
7. Order is fulfilled Friday.

## Future Improvement

Eventually, the payment process can become more automated, but the first version should prioritize clarity and reliability over technical perfection.

# 7. Recommended Order Tracker

Use a Google Sheet or Airtable first.

Do not force Square to be the full production dashboard at launch.

## Weekly Order Tracker Fields

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Customer Name | Who ordered |
| Email | Customer communication |
| Phone | Delivery or issue resolution |
| Order Date | Timing |
| Fulfillment Date | Usually Friday |
| Items Ordered | Production planning |
| Quantity | Bake count |
| Pickup/Delivery | Fulfillment planning |
| Address | Delivery only |
| Gift Message | Packaging note |
| Allergy Note | Risk control |
| Payment Status | Awaiting / Paid |
| Order Status | New / Baking / Packed / Complete |
| Notes | Anything unusual |

## Recommended Statuses

- New
- Awaiting Payment
- Paid / Confirmed
- Baking
- Packed
- Delivered / Picked Up
- Cancelled

## Key Production View

Every week, she should be able to see:

- Total brownies needed
- Total cookies needed
- Total bars needed
- Total Comfort Boxes
- Total gift boxes
- Pickup list
- Delivery list
- Gift messages
- Allergy notes

The tracker should be built for baking, not just accounting.

# 8. Recommended Capacity Model

Start intentionally small.

## Launch Capacity Recommendation

For the first few weeks, cap weekly production around:

- 10 to 20 total orders, or
- 10 to 15 Weekly Comfort Boxes, plus a few individual items, or
- A product-specific cap based on what she can bake comfortably

The purpose of the first month is not maximum revenue. It is learning the operating rhythm.

## Capacity Rule

> The business should grow only as fast as the weekly rhythm stays enjoyable and controllable.

## Capacity Questions to Monitor Weekly

- Did baking feel manageable?
- Did packing feel rushed?
- Were deliveries annoying?
- Did any product create too much mess or stress?
- Did the profit feel worth the time?
- What should be removed from next week’s menu?

# 14. Recommended Metrics

Track only what helps make better decisions.

## Weekly Metrics

- Total revenue
- Total orders
- Average order value
- Bestselling item
- Weekly Comfort Boxes sold
- Gift boxes sold
- Little Extras sold
- Repeat customers
- Unsold/wasted items
- Estimated hours worked

## Product Metrics

- Ingredient cost per batch
- Packaging cost per unit
- Pieces per batch
- Sell price
- Difficulty level
- Customer feedback
- Should repeat? Yes/no

## Decision Rule

Products should earn their place.

If an item is hard to make, low-margin, messy, and not a bestseller, it should not stay on the weekly menu.

# 16. Final Recommended Model

The Nurtured Oven should be built around a controlled, repeatable weekly rhythm.

The menu creates routine.

The Weekly Comfort Box creates the hero product.

Gift boxes create premium emotional purchases.

Little Extras reduce waste and capture late demand.

Square handles payments and reporting.

The website handles brand experience and ordering clarity.

A simple tracker handles baking and fulfillment.

The business stays strong because it does not say yes to everything.

## Final Model Statement

> The Nurtured Oven is a small-batch comfort sweets business built around a weekly preorder rhythm with menus dropping Friday. Customers order from a limited weekly menu, with the Weekly Comfort Box as the hero product, while gift boxes, seasonal drops, and Little Extras create additional revenue without disrupting production. Orders are placed through the website, confirmed through Square payment, and managed through a simple weekly production tracker so the business stays beautiful, manageable, and profitable.
